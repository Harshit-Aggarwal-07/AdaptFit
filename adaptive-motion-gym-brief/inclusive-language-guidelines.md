# Inclusive Language Guidelines

## Use

- adapted movement
- supported option
- your pace
- choose what feels safe
- strength looks different for everyone
- every body is welcome
- mobility difference
- limb difference
- accessible alternative
- movement confidence
- gentle progress
- ability profile
- areas to protect
- support preferences

## Avoid

- normal body
- abnormal body
- broken
- defective
- bad body
- fix yourself
- fat-burning shame
- perfect body
- no excuses
- beast mode
- weak
- lazy
- failed workout
- crush your weakness

## Tone

The product voice should be:

- respectful
- warm
- empowering
- calm
- non-judgmental
- body-neutral
- disability-inclusive
- trauma-aware
- confidence-building

## Example Copy

Good:

> Move only as far as feels comfortable today.

Good:

> This supported option helps you train with more stability.

Good:

> You completed movement at your own pace. That counts.

Bad:

> Push through the pain.

Bad:

> Fix your weak body.

Bad:

> No excuses.
